The sand wraith rises from the dune in a spinning column of grit that strips paint from your shield. Its form shifts between solid and storm and you cannot tell where the creature ends or wind begins.

Before you lies a frozen lake in a cavern so large that the far shore is lost in blue mist. The ice is clear enough to see dark shapes moving beneath the surface in long deliberate circles.

A spiral staircase descends into the earth and the walls grow warmer with each step you take. The stone changes from gray to rust and a faint orange glow pulses from somewhere far below.
The library stretches in every direction with shelves so tall that ladders on rails reach into the gloom. Books lie open on the tables as if their owners vanished in the middle of a quiet afternoon.
You enter a forge where the anvil still radiates heat despite the fire having gone cold long ago. Tools hang on the walls in order and a half-finished blade sits on the anvil waiting for its maker.

Bones of some colossal creature form the arched ceiling of this passage beneath the crumbling mountain roots. Its ribs curve overhead like the rafters of a great hall and moss has claimed every hollow.
The chamber holds a pool of mercury that reflects a sky of unfamiliar stars instead of the ceiling. When you lean close the reflection ripples as though something beneath the surface has noticed your presence.

Shelves of specimens line this room and each glass jar holds something stranger than the last. The liquid inside glows with faint amber light and some of the creatures still appear to be breathing.

You stand in a gallery where portraits of forgotten rulers hang in gilded frames along polished walls. Their painted eyes are startlingly lifelike and the newest portrait at the end of the row looks familiar.

You descend into a grotto where the walls are encrusted with crystals that hum when touched. A shallow stream winds through the center of the cave and its water is clear and impossibly warm.
