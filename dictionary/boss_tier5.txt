# Boss paragraphs — Tier 5 (Level 25)
Before time had a name, before the first star ignited in the primordial dark, I existed. I watched your world coalesce from dust and screaming energy. I watched the first creatures crawl from the sea, draw breath, learn fear. I taught them fear. Every nightmare that has ever woken your kind in cold sweat traces its origin to me. And you, small thing, you have come to end me? How wonderfully, tragically absurd.
I am not merely powerful. Power is a word mortals use for things they cannot hold. I am the reason power exists; the silent axiom beneath every force in this universe. Heroes before you have shattered mountains, unraveled curses that spanned millennia, rewritten the laws of death itself. They came here as legends and left as dust. Their names are forgotten. Your name will be forgotten. But I will remember the moment I erased you, for I remember everything, always, forever.
Do you feel that? That trembling in your fingers, that hollow cold behind your ribs? That is not fear of me. That is your body's ancient memory of what came before gods, before darkness had a shape. I am that memory made real. Every instinct you possess is screaming at you to run, and every instinct you possess is correct. And yet here you stand, fingers poised, breath held, ready to fight. I have not felt genuine curiosity in an age. You have earned that much from me. Now show me the rest.
Let me tell you what happens to the ones who reach me. They arrive burning with purpose, hardened by every battle that came before, certain that their journey has prepared them for this moment. And for a breath — just one — they are right. They are everything a mortal can become. Then I show them what lies beyond mortal limits, and the light goes out of their eyes. Not from pain. From understanding. I am about to show you the same truth. The only question that has ever mattered: will you flinch, or will you keep typing?
