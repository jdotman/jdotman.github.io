The dread knight raises a cursed blade and brings it crashing down toward your skull with terrible force. You twist aside and feel the wind of the strike as cracked stone erupts beneath the blow.
A colossal shadow serpent uncoils from the pit beneath the throne and fixes you with burning amber eyes. Its venomous fangs drip poison that hisses and smokes where it strikes the ancient stone floor.
The necromancer slams her staff into the ground and a wave of deathly cold sweeps through the chamber. Corpses along the walls begin to stir and reach for rusted weapons with fingers not moved in ages.
Flames erupt from the war golem as it charges with fists clenched like blackened iron rams aimed at you. You dive between its legs and slash at the glowing rune on its armored heel before it can turn.
The lich raises one skeletal hand and gravity in the room shifts violently, pinning you against the ceiling. Your sword slips from your fingers and clatters far below as the lich floats upward with cold amusement.
Black thorns burst from the earth as the corrupted druid howls and slams both fists into the cracked ground. One barbed vine wraps around your ankle and yanks you down before you can sever it with your blade.
A wyvern queen dives from the ledge and rakes the air with talons that gleam like polished dark obsidian. Her wings create a gale that sends loose stone tumbling across the floor and forces you to brace hard.
The enormous spider drops from the ceiling and lands between you and the only exit with a heavy thud. Its mandibles click as it raises two barbed forelegs and begins to circle you with terrible patience.
Frozen javelins punch through your shield and send you skidding backward across the slick stone battle floor. You wrench the dark weapon free and hurl it back but the death knight bats it aside with one hand.
Molten chains erupt from the ground and lash around your arms as the fire warden steps from the blaze. Each link burns white hot against your skin and you roar through gritted teeth while straining to pull free.
The gargoyle shatters free from the archway and descends on grinding stone wings that scrape with every beat. It lands in a crouch and opens a mouth full of granite fangs before launching itself at your throat.
Waves of psychic force slam against your mind as the elder brain drifts into the cavern on pale mist. Your vision blurs and thoughts scatter like leaves in a gale and only raw willpower keeps you standing.
The titan brings both fists down on the bridge and the entire span buckles under the devastating blow below. You leap as rubble falls beneath your boots and catch the far ledge by your fingertips over the chasm.
Bone shards erupt from skeletal archers along the high balcony above your exposed position in the ruined hall. You raise your shield and feel each impact rattle through your arm as you push forward step by step.
The blood mage opens a vein on her palm and the crimson flow twists into a whip of hardened gore. She lashes it across the gap and wraps it around your blade, nearly tearing the weapon from your grip.
Howling specters pour from every crack in the walls and swirl around you in a freezing vortex of fury. Your enchanted blade cuts through them but they reform almost instantly and each pass drains warmth from you.
The wyrm explodes from the black lake in a geyser of dark water and snaps its jaws shut near your face. You stumble backward and raise your weapon just in time to deflect its deadly second lunge at you.
Poison clouds billow from the shattered alchemist golem as it lurches toward you on one remaining leg in the ruins. Every breath burns your lungs and narrows your vision and you know you must finish this fight now.
The fallen angel spreads six tattered wings and the ground beneath her feet cracks and blackens in a widening ring. She draws a pale sword that hums with a frequency you feel in your teeth and points it at you.
Roots thick as stone pillars erupt through the flagstones and the ancient treant rises to its full towering height. Birds scatter from its canopy as it swings one massive limb with enough force to level the wall behind.
The chimera breathes green flame from its lion head while the serpent tail strikes from the opposite direction. You parry the tail with your shield and roll beneath the fire as the heat sears the back of your neck.
Spectral chains erupt from a portal in the floor and bind your arms as the revenant lord strides through mist. His rusted crown sits crooked on a skull bearing the expression of the king he once murdered long ago.
The basilisk turns its petrifying gaze upon you and you feel your boots begin to stiffen and fuse with stone. You avert your eyes and swing blindly guided only by the sound of its body dragging across the rubble.
Dark water surges through the broken dam and the leviathan rides the flood into the keep with its maw open. Its teeth are the size of longswords and its breath carries the stench of ocean trenches devoid of light.
The golem of fused armor rises from wreckage assembled from every soldier who fell in this cursed and bitter war. Each step it takes rattles with the sound of a hundred empty helmets shifting inside its hollow body.
Emerald lightning arcs from the storm drake and blasts the tower parapet into rubble raining down on the courtyard. You sprint through falling debris toward the creature knowing that one direct hit from those horns would end you.
The plague bearer shambles forward trailing clouds of yellow vapor that wilts every living thing along the corridor. Flowers carved in stone seem to curl and blacken as it passes and you wrap cloth around your face to fight.
A wall of spectral warriors appears across the bridge each one the ghost of a knight who failed this trial before. They raise translucent shields in formation and advance in lockstep and you must break their line or join them.
The vampire lord descends from the rafters in a swirl of crimson silk and lands without a sound on the floor. His smile reveals gleaming fangs as he extends one pale hand and invites you to test your steel.
All three hydra heads strike from different angles with jaws wide enough to swallow you whole in a single bite. You deflect one with your blade and dodge the second but the third clips your shoulder and spins you hard.
You stand in a cathedral whose ceiling has collapsed to reveal a sky choked with ash and drifting embers above. The pews are overturned and scorched and a massive claw mark is gouged deep into the stone altar.
The corridor ahead is carved from a single vein of dark crystal that hums when you press your hand to it. Your reflection stares back from a thousand facets but in some the reflection moves a fraction too late.
Beneath your feet stretches a floor of interlocking tiles each engraved with a rune pulsing with faint inner light. Several tiles near the center have been shattered and the darkness through the gaps suggests a vast space below.
You emerge onto a balcony overlooking an underground city built into the walls of a massive vertical shaft below. Bridges of stone and chain connect the buildings across the void and some still sway as if recently crossed.
The hallway is lined with suits of armor standing at attention in alcoves carved to fit each unique set of plate. Dust covers every surface except the floor where a single set of footprints leads to the far door ahead.
A vast mosaic covers the floor of this circular chamber depicting a battle between forces of light and dark shadow. The shadow side is cracked and heaving upward as if something beneath the floor presses against it slowly.
You enter a forge where the anvil still radiates heat despite the thick dust coating every other surface in the room. Tools hang on the walls and a half-finished blade rests in the quenching trough waiting for a lost smith.
The staircase spirals downward through layers of ancient strata exposed by the excavation of this forgotten stronghold below. You can read the ages of the earth in the bands of color on the walls beside the descent.
Massive chains hang from the ceiling and each link is as tall as a person forged from metal that resists all decay. They converge on a single point in the center of the room where only empty shackles now remain hanging.
You step into a garden grown in total darkness where pale fungi and luminescent moss have replaced flowers and grass. A stone path winds between the beds and a bench sits beside a dry fountain in the soft blue glow.
The passage opens into a scriptorium where dozens of writing desks sit in neat rows beneath oil lamps long since dark. Unfinished manuscripts cover every desk and ink has dried in the wells frozen by whatever calamity emptied this place.
You stand in a chamber dominated by a massive stone sarcophagus covered in carved warnings written in three old languages. The lid has been shifted aside just enough to peer within and scratch marks on the inside tell the rest.
The room is perfectly spherical and carved with geometric precision that seems impossible for tools of this ancient era. Sound behaves strangely here with whispers amplified to shouts and shouts dampened to whispers at every step.
A bridge of natural stone spans a chasm so deep that dropping a torch reveals nothing but a fading orange point. The bridge is only three paces wide with no railing and the wind from the depths is constant and cold.
You enter a reliquary where sacred objects rest on velvet cushions inside cases of thick glass yellowed by long ages. Some cases have been smashed but others hold items that glow or hum or hover above their cushions still.
The tunnel narrows until you must turn sideways to squeeze between walls pressing against your chest and back tightly. After twenty paces the passage opens suddenly into a vast cavern where torchlight cannot reach the ceiling above.
You stand in a war room with a massive table at its center upon which rests a terrain model of unknown lands. Tiny carved figures represent armies and someone has recently moved several pieces toward a mountain fortress nearby.
The chamber is filled with an ankle deep layer of bones that shift and clatter beneath your boots with each step. They are the remains of countless creatures and the sheer volume suggests centuries of disposal in this dark pit.
A waterfall of molten rock pours from a fissure high in the cavern wall and feeds a river of lava below. The only way across is a series of stone pillars rising from the flow each barely wide enough to stand on.
The crypt extends in every direction with low ceilings supported by pillars carved to resemble hooded figures in prayer. Between the pillars lie stone coffins in concentric circles radiating from a central tomb larger than all the rest.
You find yourself in a hall of mirrors where every surface reflects your image in slightly different poses and expressions. Some reflections carry weapons you do not possess and one appears to mouth words you cannot hear at all.
The passage leads through a cave where stalactites and stalagmites have grown together into pale mineral columns over long ages. Water drips steadily from above and has carved shallow channels in the floor leading deeper into the mountain.
Beyond the archway lies a perfect cube of polished black stone with no visible exits besides the one you entered through. A pedestal stands in the center holding a glass sphere that contains a miniature storm crackling with lightning.
You descend into a flooded temple where the water reaches your waist and ancient statues rise from the murky surface. Fish dart between submerged pillars and the water is so clear near the bottom you can read the drowned inscriptions.
The corridor walls are covered in tally marks scratched into stone by fingernails layered over each other in frantic clusters. Near the end the marks become words then sentences then a desperate account of imprisonment that ends mid-thought.
You emerge into a vast cavern where the ceiling is encrusted with crystals that emit a soft and steady blue glow. The light is bright enough to see by and casts no shadows giving the space an ethereal dreamlike quality.
A throne of twisted iron sits atop a dais of cracked obsidian in a hall bearing centuries of magical battle scars. Scorch marks and frost patches and acid stains overlap in layers that tell the story of each challenger before.
The passageway opens onto a ledge high inside a hollow mountain where a fortress has been carved from the interior rock. Towers and battlements protrude at impossible angles connected by staircases that seem to defy all structural reason.
You enter a chamber where time moves differently evidenced by a candle burning at one end while its twin stands frozen. Dust motes hang motionless near the walls but swirl normally in the center of the strange still room.
