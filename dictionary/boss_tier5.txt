The banshee screams and the sound drives you violently down to your trembling knees. You grit your teeth and force yourself back upright, ears ringing and your legs barely holding.
The cursed knight charges from across the great hall with impossible, blinding speed. You barely manage to sidestep the lance before it buries itself deep into the cold stone wall.
Dark runes flare to blazing life along the walls of the summoning chamber below. Whatever the cultists have called forth is far more powerful than anything you were expecting today.
The necromancer raises his staff and the ground splits open beneath you. Skeletal hands claw at your boots as you scramble backward, slashing wildly to keep your footing on the crumbling stone.
From the heart of the volcano, the magma titan rises in a shower of molten rock and ash. The air itself seems to burn as it turns its smoldering gaze upon you and takes one thundering step forward.
The warlock completes his dark ritual and a portal tears open in the sky above the battlefield. Something vast and terrible peers through, and you realize this fight just became far more dangerous.
The shadow lord spreads his cloak and the room plunges into absolute darkness around you. You can hear him moving but cannot see him, and every instinct screams at you to strike before he does.
A chorus of howls rises from the forest behind you as the werewolf alpha steps into the moonlit clearing. Its pack circles in the trees, waiting for the signal to attack from every direction at once.
The fallen paladin kneels before the dark altar and draws strength from the cursed relic embedded in it. When he stands again, his eyes burn with unholy fire and his armor crackles with dark energy.
The titan slams its stone fist into the ground and a shockwave throws you off your feet and into a wall. You taste blood, push yourself upright, and raise your weapon again with shaking hands.
The lich queen floats above her frozen throne, her voice echoing from every wall of the vast and empty chamber. She has waited three hundred years for a worthy challenger to find this hidden place.
The demon general draws a blade of black flame and points it at your heart with slow, deliberate menace. Behind him, his army stretches to the horizon, and every single one of them is watching you.
The clockwork dragon unfolds its bronze wings and gears grind to life inside its ancient chest. Steam hisses from its joints as it locks its glowing crystal eyes onto you and begins to advance.
You step through the final gate and find the dragon coiled around a mountain of gold and bone. One eye opens slowly, fixes on you, and you see in it an intelligence far older than any human kingdom.
The storm witch raises both hands and lightning arcs between her fingers in a blinding web of light. Rain lashes your face as the wind howls, and you know you must reach her before the next bolt falls.
The bone colossus rises from the ancient graveyard, pulling tombstones and iron fences into its mass as it grows taller. Each step sends tremors through the earth, and the dead beneath the soil stir in response to its terrible call.
The flame warden blocks the bridge with a wall of fire that stretches from one cliff face to the other. Its eyes are two bright coals burning inside a helmet of blackened steel, and it has stood this post since the last age.
The swamp titan drags itself from the mire on arms thick with tangled roots and hanging moss. The water churns behind it as smaller creatures flee, and the stench of ancient decay rolls over you like a physical wave.
The war sphinx settles on the ruined temple steps and curls its tail around its stone paws with deliberate grace. It asks you a question in a language you almost understand, and you sense that a wrong answer would be far worse than silence.
The crystal hydra raises three heads of faceted gemstone, each one refracting the torchlight into blinding rainbow beams. When you sever one head, the stump splits and regrows two more, each sharper and brighter than the one before it.
The siege wurm bursts from the castle foundation and the entire east tower begins to lean and crumble above you. Soldiers scatter as the creature coils through the rubble, its armored hide scraping against stone with a sound like grinding teeth.
The iron revenant drags its chained greatsword across the dungeon floor, leaving a trail of white sparks behind it. It was executed here centuries ago, and the fury of its unjust death has kept it standing ever since that dark day.
The venom queen descends from her web of silk and poison on eight silent, razor-tipped legs. Her fangs drip with a paralytic that could stop your heart in seconds, and she moves with the patience of a predator that has never missed.
The lightning drake dives from the storm clouds and rakes the hillside with talons that crackle with raw voltage. The ground where it strikes turns to glass, and the thunder that follows is so loud you feel it in your ribs and in your teeth.
The puppet lich pulls the strings of a dozen skeletal warriors and sends them at you in a coordinated wave. Each one moves with the skill it had in life, and the lich watches from behind their ranks with cold, appraising interest.
The magma golem hauls itself from the volcanic vent, each movement sending rivers of molten rock cascading down its shoulders. The heat is so intense that your leather armor begins to smoke, and you know a single touch would be the end.
The frozen knight stands at the heart of the blizzard, ice crystals orbiting its still form like a slow and deadly crown. It raises a sword made of compressed frost and the temperature plunges so sharply that your fingers go numb on your weapon.
The ghost admiral floats above the deck of his sunken ship as it rises from the harbor floor in a rush of dark water. His spectral crew takes their stations with mechanical precision, and the cannons of the dead vessel turn slowly toward the shore.
The abyssal watcher emerges from the cave mouth on a dozen limbs of shadow and stone, its single vast eye sweeping the valley. Whatever it sees, it remembers, and whatever it remembers, it hunts, and it has been watching you since you entered this cursed land.
You are standing in a vast underground harbor. Stone docks line a black river that flows silently into the dark, and rotting boats are still tied to their moorings with fraying rope.
The corridor opens into a cathedral of ice. Frozen columns rise from floor to ceiling, and the light from your torch refracts through them, casting blue and white patterns across every surface.
You have entered a room that appears to be a map of the world, carved in painstaking relief into the stone floor. Several regions have been scratched out violently, and you notice yours is among them.
The passage ends at a vast sinkhole. A spiral staircase of crumbling stone hugs the inner wall, descending into a darkness so complete that your torchlight cannot find the bottom.
You step into an overgrown courtyard. A fountain stands at the center, still flowing with water that is perfectly clear despite the ruin around it, as if something keeps it running.
The chamber is filled with enormous gears and bronze mechanisms that have long since stopped turning. Dust coats every surface, and the air smells of oil and old metal and forgotten purpose.
You are in a library carved into the living rock. The shelves are stone ledges and the books are metal plates etched with text in a language you have never seen before.
The tunnel opens onto a cliff face overlooking a vast subterranean ocean. Bioluminescent creatures drift far below like slow, blue stars moving through a sky made of black water.
You enter a throne room split in half by a massive crack in the floor. One side has collapsed into the earth below, and the throne itself teeters on the edge of the gap.
The walls of this corridor are covered in murals depicting the rise and fall of a civilization you do not recognize. The final panel shows a door that looks exactly like the one ahead of you.
You stand in an ancient amphitheater open to the night sky. The stone seats are cracked and overgrown, but the acoustics are so perfect that your breathing echoes back to you.
The path leads through a petrified forest. Every tree has turned to gray stone, frozen mid-sway as if a single terrible moment turned the entire woodland to rock in an instant.
You are in a domed observatory. The ceiling has been painted with star charts that do not match any sky you have ever seen, and a brass telescope points at a patch of wall, not sky.
The chamber ahead is flooded with ankle-deep water that is unnaturally warm. Steam rises from the surface and the walls are slick with condensation that glows faintly green in the dark.
You descend into a mine shaft reinforced with ancient timbers. The cart tracks on the floor lead deeper into the mountain, and a faint rhythmic tapping echoes from somewhere far ahead.
You are standing in a vault whose walls are lined with deposit boxes, each sealed with a different lock of increasing complexity. Several have been pried open and their contents are scattered on the floor, but the largest box at the far end remains sealed.
The corridor opens into a greenhouse built beneath a skylight of thick glass. The plants have overgrown their beds and climbed the walls, and some have reached the glass and begun to crack it with the slow persistence of roots.
You enter a chamber that serves as a junction between four tunnels, each one sloping at a different angle. A compass rose is carved into the floor but the directions do not match the tunnels, and one of the four cardinal points has been deliberately chiseled away.
The passage leads through a gallery of death masks hung on the walls in rows that stretch from one end of the long hall to the other. Each mask is different and each bears an expression of calm acceptance, except for the last three, which show increasing stages of alarm.
You are in a barracks carved into the rock. The bunks are stone shelves stacked three high, and personal effects are still tucked into the niches beside each bed, as if the soldiers simply left one morning and never came back.
The tunnel opens into a natural cathedral of limestone, its ceiling hung with formations that have been growing for millions of years. A path has been worn through the center by generations of pilgrims, and offerings are stacked at the base of the largest column.
You step into a foundry where the crucibles are still full of solidified metal that cooled in the shapes of whatever was being poured when the workers abandoned their stations. The molds on the shelves are for objects you do not recognize.
The chamber is a theater with a stage at one end and rows of stone seats rising in tiers. The curtain is still drawn and the stage is set for a performance, with props and scenery arranged for a play whose title is written above the proscenium in a dead language.
You are standing in a navigation room. Star charts cover the walls and a massive orrery of brass and glass sits on the central table, its planets still turning in slow orbits powered by a mechanism hidden inside the base that has not wound down in centuries.
The passageway leads to a garden built inside a courtyard that was sealed off when the upper floors collapsed centuries ago. Rain still finds its way in through gaps in the rubble above, and the garden has flourished in its stone prison, wild and untended and utterly beautiful.
You enter a room that was once a nursery. Small beds line the walls and toys are scattered across the floor, carved wooden animals and cloth dolls that have survived remarkably well in the dry underground air.
The corridor ends at a set of double doors made of a metal you cannot identify. They are cold to the touch even in the warmth of the underground, and the handles are shaped like clasped hands that you must pull apart to open.
You are in a room full of clocks, hundreds of them, covering every surface and hanging from every beam. None of them show the same time and all of them are running, filling the room with a constant overlapping ticking that makes it impossible to think clearly.
The tunnel opens into a canyon where a river once flowed but has long since dried up. The smooth stones on the canyon floor are arranged in spirals by a current that no longer exists, and the walls are carved with high-water marks from ancient floods.
You step into a courtyard where a massive sundial takes up the entire floor. The gnomon casts a shadow that points to a symbol you do not recognize, and you notice the shadow moves even though the sun is not visible from this deep underground.
